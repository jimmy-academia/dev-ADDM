# Kitchen Performance Assessment

Help assess the kitchen performance at this restaurant using customer reviews as evidence.
This is from the perspective of a business owner evaluating their kitchen team's execution.
Assign a performance label: Needs Improvement, Satisfactory, or Excellent.

## Definitions of Terms

### What to Consider (Guidance)

Consider reviews that mention any of the following:
- food quality issues (temperature, doneness, taste)
- consistency of food quality across visits
- presentation and plating
- chef skill level
- food sent back or complaints addressed

Reviews that only discuss service without mentioning food quality carry less weight.

*Note: The above describes what types of content are relevant for this task. It is NOT a field to extract.*

### Account Type
How the reviewer relates to the described event

- **firsthand**: The reviewer or their dining party experienced the event directly (e.g., "I had...", "my child...")
- **secondhand**: The reviewer reports someone else's experience (e.g., "my friend...", "I heard...")
- **hypothetical**: Concern or preference without describing an actual incident (e.g., "I'm allergic so I worry...")

### Food Issue Type
Type of food quality issue

- **temperature**: Food served at wrong temperature
- **doneness**: Food over or undercooked
- **taste**: Flavor issues (bland, too salty, etc.)
- **presentation**: Visual presentation issues
- **portion**: Portion size issues
- **none**: No food issues mentioned

### Issue Severity
Severity of food quality issues

- **none**: No issues, food was good
- **minor**: Small issues, still edible
- **major**: Significant issues affecting enjoyment
- **inedible**: Food was not edible

### Consistency
Consistency of food quality

- **inconsistent**: Quality varies significantly between visits/dishes
- **variable**: Some variation but generally acceptable
- **consistent**: Reliable quality every time

### Chef Skill
Perceived skill level of kitchen

- **poor**: Kitchen seems unskilled
- **average**: Competent but unremarkable
- **good**: Skilled kitchen, quality execution
- **excellent**: Exceptional culinary skill

### Food Sent Back
Whether food was sent back

- **true**: Food was sent back to kitchen
- **false**: Food was not sent back


## Verdict Rules

A restaurant is considered **Excellent** if any of the following is true:
- 55 or more reviews praise excellent chef skill
- 90 or more reviews report consistent food quality

**Needs Improvement** if Excellent does not apply, but any of the following is true:
- 28 or more reviews report poor chef skill
- 32 or more reviews report inconsistent food quality

**Satisfactory** otherwise, especially when:
- Food is generally good with occasional issues
- Competent execution without exceptional creativity
- Some standout dishes, some average
