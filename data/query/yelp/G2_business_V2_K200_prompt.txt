# Business Dining Suitability Assessment

Help assess how suitable this restaurant is for business dining (client meetings, work lunches, interviews)
using customer reviews as evidence.
Assign a suitability label: Not Recommended, Acceptable, or Recommended.

This assessment uses a point-based scoring system to quantify suitability.

## Definitions of Terms

### Incident

Consider reviews that mention any of the following:
- business lunches, client meetings, interviews, work events
- noise level and ability to have conversation
- service efficiency and professionalism
- privacy for business discussions
- overall professional atmosphere

Reviews that only discuss food without mentioning the business context carry less weight.

### Account Type
How the reviewer relates to the described event

- **Firsthand**: The reviewer or their dining party experienced the event directly (e.g., "I had...", "my child...")
- **Secondhand**: The reviewer reports someone else's experience (e.g., "my friend...", "I heard...")
- **Hypothetical**: Concern or preference without describing an actual incident (e.g., "I'm allergic so I worry...")

### Professionalism
Overall professional atmosphere

- **Poor**: Unprofessional, inappropriate for business
- **Adequate**: Acceptable for casual business, not formal
- **Good**: Professional atmosphere, suitable for most business
- **Excellent**: Highly professional, suitable for important clients

### Conversation Suitability
Suitability for business conversation

- **Poor**: Too loud or distracting for business discussion
- **Fair**: Possible to talk but some challenges
- **Good**: Comfortable for business conversation
- **Excellent**: Perfect for confidential or important discussions

### Service Efficiency
Service efficiency for busy professionals

- **Slow**: Service too slow for business meal
- **Normal**: Average pace, not ideal for quick meetings
- **Efficient**: Quick and professional service
- **Exemplary**: Exceptional timing, understands business needs

### Privacy Level
Privacy available for business discussions

- **None**: Very public, no privacy for sensitive topics
- **Low**: Limited privacy, must be careful what is discussed
- **Moderate**: Reasonable privacy for most business talk
- **High**: Private rooms or very secluded tables available

### Meeting Outcome
How the business meal/meeting went

- **Negative**: Restaurant negatively impacted the meeting
- **Neutral**: Restaurant was adequate, didn't stand out
- **Positive**: Restaurant contributed positively to the meeting


## Scoring System

Calculate a business suitability score by summing points from relevant reviews.
Positive points indicate business suitability, negative points indicate drawbacks.

For each firsthand incident, assign points based on severity:
- Successful client meeting: 15 points
- Positive business meal: 5 points
- Neutral mention: 0 points
- Minor issue (slightly loud, slow): -2 points
- Major issue (too loud, very slow): -5 points
- Client embarrassment: -15 points

Additional modifiers:
- Private room available (mentions private dining room or secluded area): +5 points
- Quiet atmosphere (explicitly describes as quiet or easy to talk): +3 points
- Efficient service (service described as quick and professional): +3 points
- Loud environment (noise level prevents easy conversation): -5 points
- Slow service (service too slow for business timing): -3 points


## Verdict Rules

A restaurant is considered **Recommended** if any of the following is true:
- Total suitability score is 1200 or higher

**Not Recommended** if Recommended does not apply, but any of the following is true:
- Total suitability score is 0 or lower

**Acceptable** otherwise, especially when:
- Total suitability score is between 1 and 1199
- Mixed positive and negative indicators
